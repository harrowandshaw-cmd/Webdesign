I · MANIFESTO

Shoemaking, as practised
at the bench.

A Meher shoe is not a fast object. It arrives slowly — through eighty-three hours of hand-work, two hundred and seven pieces, thirty-one operations — and leaves, we hope, even more slowly. Our makers are expected to be patient, their tools sharp, their days quiet. What they produce is meant to last a wearer twenty years at ordinary use, resoled four or five times, and then — if all goes well — passed on.
— Abdul Rehman Meher
FOUNDER · MAKER N° 01 · 1967
II · THE COLLECTION

Seven shapes, refined
across forty years.

Our standing range of seven lasts, each named for a river of the Punjab — Ravi, Jhelum, Chenab, Sutlej, Beas, Indus, Kabul. Every pair is Goodyear-welted, finished by hand, and available in calf, shell cordovan or suede. Ready-to-wear or bespoke.

III · THE CRAFT

A shoe, cut open.

Every Meher shoe is hand-welted in the Goodyear method — a construction favoured for two hundred years because it allows a shoe to be resoled without disturbing the upper. Seven layers, one narrow strip of oak-tanned welt, and a single honest stitch. Click each point to walk through it.

PART N° 02

The Welt

A narrow strip of oak-tanned cow leather, hand-stitched to the upper and the insole rib in the Goodyear method. The welt is the single feature that decides whether a shoe can be honestly resoled — four or five times, at a skilled cobbler, without ever disturbing the upper.

From hide to hand,
in seven chapters.

Scroll → each chapter is a day or more at the bench. The whole takes three weeks for a ready-to-wear pair; eighteen months for a bespoke one.

The closing room.
Quarters, vamp, tongue and counter are stitched together at seven-to-the-inch with waxed linen thread.

The welting.
At the bench, a single strip of welt is stitched to upper and insole rib — 148 hand-driven stitches per shoe.

Oak-bark tanned outsole, stitched to the welt. Cork filler set between; heel stacked in six layers of leather.

Burnished with deer-bone, waxed, and hot-ironed. The pair rests two days before it is boxed with its shoe trees.

A craft, older
than the country
it is made in.

Sialkot has tanned and cut leather for over four hundred years — it is our city of kaarigar, master craftsmen whose fathers taught them, and whose fathers taught them. Abdul Rehman Meher opened a three-man bench here in 1967, the year after his apprenticeship ended. Today his son, Faisal Meher, runs the same forty-man workshop from the same street.

We are the first Pakistani house to hand-welt dress shoes in the Northampton manner — not as imitation, but because we believe Sialkot craftsmanship deserves a shoe at that level. Everything is cut, closed, lasted, welted, soled and finished under one roof on Daula Street.

VII · BESPOKE

A pair, made to the
shape of you.

Commissioning a bespoke Meher pair is a slow conversation. You will sit with Faisal at our Karachi or London atelier for a first fitting, return six weeks later for the trial shoe, and receive your finished pair roughly fourteen months later. Your last — a wooden sculpture of your foot — remains with us for life, so every subsequent pair takes only half the time.
