III · The Makers

Forty-two kaarigar,
one workshop
floor.

Sialkot has tanned and cut leather for over four hundred years. Abdul Rehman Meher opened a three-man bench on Daula Street in 1967, the year after his apprenticeship ended. Today his son, Faisal Meher, runs the same forty-two-person workshop from the same street — still with no outsourced operations, still with every shoe cut, closed, welted and soled under one roof.

We were the first Pakistani house to hand-welt dress shoes in the Northampton manner. Not as imitation, but because we believe Sialkot craftsmanship deserves a shoe at that level.

IV · The Process

From hide to hand,
in seven chapters.

Scroll horizontally. Each chapter is a day — or more — at the bench. The whole takes three weeks for a ready-to-wear pair; fourteen months for a bespoke one.

CH · 01
01
DAY 01 · 06 HRS
The hide, chosen.
Full French calf, or shell cordovan from Horween, Chicago. Inspected against window light for grain, scar and temper.
CH · 02
02
DAY 02 · 02 HRS
The last, readied.
Beechwood last N° 402, seasoned twelve months before it meets leather. Cleaned, waxed and chalked for grip.
CH · 03
03
DAY 03 · 09 HRS
The clicker's bench.
Ustad Rizwan cuts eighteen pattern pieces from the hide with a bone-handled knife. One hide gives three pairs — no more.
CH · 04
04
DAY 04–06 · 14 HRS
The closing room.
Quarters, vamp, tongue and counter are stitched together at seven-to-the-inch with waxed linen thread, by the closer's fingertips.
CH · 05
05
DAY 09–11 · 16 HRS
The welting.
A single strip of oak-tanned welt, hand-stitched to upper and insole rib — 148 stitches, each driven with an awl and a hooked needle.
CH · 06
06
DAY 14 · 08 HRS
The sole, laid.
Oak-bark tanned outsole stitched to the welt. Cork filler hand-packed between. Heel stacked in six leather layers.
CH · 07
07
DAY 18–21 · 12 HRS
The finish.
Burnished with deer bone, waxed, hot-ironed. Rests two days before it is boxed with its shoe trees.

VI · Manifesto
A Meher shoe arrives slowly — through eighty-three hours of hand-work, two hundred and seven pieces, thirty-one operations — and leaves, we hope, even more slowly. Our makers are expected to be patient, their tools sharp, their days quiet. What they produce is meant to last a wearer twenty years at ordinary use, resoled four or five times, and then, if all goes well, passed on.
SIGNED
Abdul Rehman Meher

VII · Bespoke

A pair, made to
the shape of you.

Commissioning a bespoke Meher pair is a slow conversation. You sit with Faisal at our Karachi or London atelier for a first fitting, return six weeks later for the trial shoe, and receive your finished pair roughly fourteen months later. Your wooden last remains with us for life — every subsequent pair then takes half the time.
